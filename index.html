    Our Cause

    Our cause is about protecting the enviromental integrity of Georgia and promoting it's biodiversity.

    Environmental Facts About Georgia

    Fun Environmental Facts

    • Biodiversity Hotspot: Georgia is home to over 4,000 plant species and 300 bird species. The country is a biodiversity hotspot because of its variety of ecosystems, including the Caucasus Mountains.
    • National Parks and Protected Areas: About 8% of Georgia's land is protected. This includes areas like Tusheti National Park, which is one of the most pristine regions in the country.
    • Vineyard Tradition: Georgia is considered the birthplace of wine. Its winemaking history spans over 8,000 years.
    • Sustainable Hydroelectric Potential: Georgia has significant potential for renewable energy, particularly hydropower, due to its mountainous terrain.

    Pollution Issues in Georgia

    • Air Pollution: Tbilisi, the capital, suffers from high air pollution, largely due to vehicle emissions and industrial activity.
    • Water Pollution: Rivers like the Rioni River are contaminated by agricultural runoff, untreated sewage, and industrial waste.
    • Waste Management: Georgia faces a growing waste management problem, with limited recycling and reliance on landfills for waste disposal.
    • Deforestation: Logging and agricultural expansion have led to significant deforestation, particularly in the Caucasus Mountains.

      • Solutions for a Greener Georgia

      • Promote Renewable Energy: Expanding solar and wind energy could help Georgia reduce its reliance on fossil fuels.
      • Improved Public Transport and Green Infrastructure: Developing efficient public transport and encouraging electric vehicles can help reduce air pollution in urban areas.
      • Waste Management and Recycling Programs: Introducing better waste collection and recycling systems would reduce pollution and support a circular economy.
      • Forest Conservation and Reforestation: Stronger regulations and reforestation projects can help restore degraded lands and protect biodiversity.
      • Stronger Environmental Regulations: Enforcing pollution control measures in industries and improving waste treatment can protect air and water quality.
      • Eco-Tourism Development: Promoting eco-tourism would help preserve Georgia's natural beauty and raise awareness about environmental issues.

    By focusing on these efforts, we hope to make a positive change in Georgia and create a sustainable future for its people and natural ecosystems.
